# ARGUS Demo Video — Script (3:30)

Timed at ~150 words/minute spoken pace (~520 words total). Each beat lists
screen-recording direction alongside the voiceover line so it can be shot and
cut directly against this script.

---

**0:00–0:15 — HOOK**
VISUAL: Black screen → fade in India outline map → Brent price ticking up.
VO: *"India imports eighty-eight percent of its crude oil. Forty percent of that sails through one strait — Hormuz. When geopolitics moves, this isn't theoretical. It's Tuesday."*

**0:15–0:35 — WHAT ARGUS IS**
VISUAL: Cut to War Room globe, slowly rotating, risk-colored markers glowing.
VO: *"This is ARGUS — an AI-driven energy resilience system built for exactly this. Real news, real prices, real infrastructure data feed a neuro-symbolic core: the LLM only reads headlines and writes briefings. Every risk score, every dollar, every barrel is computed by deterministic math you can audit."*

**0:35–1:00 — RISK ENGINE (corridor + supplier)**
VISUAL: War Room → click Hormuz marker, risk panel opens. Cut to Network page, supplier list, Russia's risk badge.
VO: *"Every fifteen minutes, ARGUS pulls live headlines, extracts corridor and supplier signals, and Bayesian-updates disruption risk — not just by chokepoint, but by supplier. Right now Hormuz sits at thirty percent, Russia at eight — both moving on real, corroborated evidence you can click through and read."*

**1:00–1:35 — SCENARIO CONSOLE (chokepoint closure)**
VISUAL: Scenario Console → set Hormuz, 60% severity → click Run → stat tiles fill in one by one → briefing text types out.
VO: *"Pick a scenario — a sixty percent Hormuz closure — and ARGUS runs a thousand Monte Carlo simulations in under a second. Peak Brent, retail petrol pass-through, GDP hit, and — new this round — power sector stress, because a refinery run-cut squeezes India's diesel backup capacity too. Managed versus unmanaged response, side by side, so the value of acting fast is a number, not a slogan."*

**1:35–2:00 — PROCUREMENT LP REALISM**
VISUAL: Scroll to order sheet — highlight Russia split across two routes instead of one.
VO: *"The replacement order sheet isn't a guess — it's a linear program pricing the cheapest feasible barrels, constrained by real tanker availability and port discharge capacity. Watch it split Russian volume across two routes instead of dumping it on one — because in a real crisis, ships and berths are the actual bottleneck."*

**2:00–2:20 — OPEC+ CUT (differentiator)**
VISUAL: Toggle "OPEC+ production cut" → Run → highlight exposure % and excluded-suppliers list.
VO: *"Switch shock types entirely — an OPEC+ emergency cut instead of a corridor closure. Different mechanism, same engine: eighty-five percent of imports exposed, every OPEC+ supplier excluded from relief, because they're the ones cutting."*

**2:20–2:45 — SPR BRIDGE + REPLENISHMENT**
VISUAL: Strategic reserve bridge section — drawdown line, then the new replenishment line.
VO: *"Strategic reserves bridge the gap — how many barrels, over how many days, from which caverns. And for the first time, ARGUS also models the replenishment window: when refill purchasing starts, how long it takes to get the reserve back to full before the next crisis hits."*

**2:45–3:05 — RIGOR / TRANSPARENCY**
VISUAL: Assumptions page — drag a slider, hit Apply. Cut to backtest results on Intelligence/Sources.
VO: *"Every number traces back to a sourced, confidence-rated assumption you can edit live. And it's not just theory — ARGUS replays five real historical crises and measures its own lead time against what actually happened."*

**3:05–3:25 — BREADTH / CLOSE**
VISUAL: Quick cuts — Network 3D globe, Global Context page, response clock ticking (0.3s).
VO: *"Twenty-two refineries, eight suppliers, ten shipping corridors, modeled end to end — from raw headline to executable order sheet in under three seconds. This is what turns a crisis from reactive to anticipated."*

**3:25–3:30 — TAG**
VISUAL: ARGUS wordmark, tagline.
VO: *"ARGUS. Energy security, computed."*

## Production notes
- Total: 210s script against a 210s (3:30) target — no trimming needed if delivered at a measured pace.
- Every screen shown is a real, live page in the running app — no mockups needed.
- If you need to cut for time: the OPEC+ cut beat (2:00–2:20) is the safest trim since chokepoint closure already demonstrates the scenario engine; cutting it drops the video to ~3:10.
